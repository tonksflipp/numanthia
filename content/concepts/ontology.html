Ontology

Ontology is the branch of philosophy that studies the nature of existence, being, and reality. It explores questions about what entities exist and what it means for something to exist. Ontologists seek to categorize and understand different types of beings, such as physical objects, abstract concepts, and mental states. The field also examines the relationships between these entities and their properties.
