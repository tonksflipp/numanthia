Nihilism

Nihilism is the philosophical stance that life lacks inherent meaning, value, or truth. It confronts the unsettling possibility that our deepest beliefs—about morality, purpose, or identity—may be unfounded or illusions. Rather than offering comfort, nihilism exposes the void beneath systems we often take for granted: religion, tradition, reason. It asks: What if nothing truly matters? This question can feel paralyzing, but for some, it opens the door to radical freedom and the chance to build new values from scratch. Associated with thinkers like Friedrich Nietzsche, nihilism is not always passive or despairing. Nietzsche warned of the dangers of nihilism—and challenged us to move through it, creating meaning through strength, creativity, and will. Nihilism doesn’t hand us answers. It hands us the silence after the old answers fall away.
