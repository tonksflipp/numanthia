Existentialism

Existentialism is a philosophical movement that centers on the individual's experience in a world that may lack inherent meaning. Instead of offering universal truths, it asks us to confront the freedom—and burden—of creating our own purpose. Rooted in 19th and 20th-century thought, existentialism explores themes like freedom, anxiety, authenticity, and the absurd. It challenges us to live genuinely, make conscious choices, and take full responsibility for who we become—even in the face of uncertainty, isolation, or despair. Thinkers like Søren Kierkegaard, Friedrich Nietzsche, Jean-Paul Sartre, and Simone de Beauvoir shaped this school of thought, each offering unique responses to the central question: What does it mean to exist? In existentialism, meaning isn’t given—it’s made.It invites us to embrace the tension between our desire for meaning and a silent universe. In doing so, existentialism becomes not just a philosophy, but a way of being.
