Schools

This section will introduce different philosophical schools and traditions.

  • Stoicism

    A philosophical school that promotes virtue as the path to happiness.

  • Epicureanism

    Founded by Epicurus, it promotes the pursuit of pleasure and the absence of pain as the primary goal of life.

  • Platonism

    The philosophical school based on the ideas of Plato, which advocates the existence of a world of perfect ideas.

  • Aristotelianism

    The philosophy of Aristotle, which emphasizes logic, ethics, and empirical science as methods of knowledge.

  • Pessimism

    The school that develops the idea that human suffering is inherent to existence.

  • Nihilism

    A philosophical stance that denies inherent meaning or value in life, often confronting the collapse of traditional beliefs.

  • Existentialism

    A philosophy focused on individual freedom, authenticity, and the creation of meaning in an indifferent or absurd world.
